How to design the noun phrases (for SAM3 text prompts):

1. Each phrase must be:
   - A concrete, visual noun phrase (object or region), not an abstract concept.
   - Segmentable as a contiguous region in the image.
   - At most 8–12 words.
   - In English.

2. Good examples:
   - "front left green chair"
   - "bouquet of pink roses in vase"
   - "long wooden dining table"
   - "round pendant light above table"
   - "geometric patterned tile backsplash"
   - "white ceramic bowls on dining table"
   - "dark wooden kitchen cabinets"

3. Bad examples (AVOID):
   - Pure attributes only: "warm lighting", "cozy atmosphere".
   - Only actions: "person sitting and talking", "someone walking".
   - Only text / numbers written on signs: "word coffee on the mug", "number 8 on ball".
   - Too vague: "stuff on the table", "area on the right".

4. You SHOULD:
   - Prefer whole objects instead of tiny parts, unless the part is clearly a meaningful target (e.g., "door handle", "sink faucet").
   - Use simple spatial qualifiers when helpful: "front", "back", "left", "right", "nearest", "farthest", "center".
   - Use visual attributes: color, material, shape, coarse location: e.g. "green dining chair", "round wooden coffee table".
   - Include important surfaces or regions: "hexagonal tiled floor", "concrete kitchen countertop", "large window on right wall".

5. You SHOULD AVOID:
   - Overly long, sentence-like descriptions.
   - Referring expressions that depend on reasoning across time (no temporal logic).
   - Very thin or extremely small details unless they are visually salient and meaningful.

6. Coverage & diversity:
   - Aim to propose between 8 and 15 prompts.
   - Cover different semantic types (furniture, tableware, decorations, structural elements, etc.) when present.
   - Avoid near-duplicates like:
     - "green chair in front"
     - "front green chair"
     - Only keep one of them.

--------------------------------
Step-by-step guide for your <think> block:

In the <think> block you should:
1) Describe the global scene layout (foreground, midground, background).
2) List candidate objects that are visually distinct and segmentable.
3) For each candidate, briefly judge:
   - Is it a solid object or coherent region?
   - Is it big enough and clear in the image?
   - Is it semantically meaningful?
4) Filter out bad candidates (pure textures, shadows, reflections, vague regions).
5) Rewrite the remaining good candidates into short, SAM3-friendly noun phrases as defined above.
6) Decide the final list (8–15 phrases).

DO NOT put any JSON, code, or Python list in the <think> block.
ONLY the <prompt_list> block should contain the Python list.
